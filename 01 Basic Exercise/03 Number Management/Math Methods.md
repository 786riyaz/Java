## **1. Basic Arithmetic Helpers**
* **Math.abs(x)** – Returns the absolute (positive) value of x.
* **Math.max(a, b)** – Returns the larger of two values.
* **Math.min(a, b)** – Returns the smaller of two values.
* **Math.pow(a, b)** – Returns a raised to the power b.
* **Math.sqrt(x)** – Returns the square root of x.
* **Math.cbrt(x)** – Returns the cube root of x.
---
## **2. Rounding Functions**
* **Math.round(x)** – Rounds to nearest integer (long/ int).
* **Math.ceil(x)** – Returns the smallest integer ≥ x.
* **Math.floor(x)** – Returns the largest integer ≤ x.
* **Math.rint(x)** – Returns nearest integer as double (ties round to even).
* **Math.random()** – Returns a random double between 0.0 and 1.0.
---
## **3. Trigonometric Functions**
* **Math.sin(x)** – Sine of angle x (in radians).
* **Math.cos(x)** – Cosine of angle x.
* **Math.tan(x)** – Tangent of angle x.
* **Math.asin(x)** – Inverse sine (returns radians).
* **Math.acos(x)** – Inverse cosine (returns radians).
* **Math.atan(x)** – Inverse tangent (returns radians).
* **Math.atan2(y, x)** – Angle between x-axis and point (x, y).
---
## **4. Exponential & Logarithmic**
* **Math.exp(x)** – Returns e raised to power x.
* **Math.log(x)** – Natural logarithm (base e).
* **Math.log10(x)** – Logarithm base 10.
* **Math.log1p(x)** – log(1 + x) (accurate for very small x).
---
## **5. Angle Conversion**
* **Math.toRadians(deg)** – Converts degrees to radians.
* **Math.toDegrees(rad)** – Converts radians to degrees.
---
## **6. Hyperbolic Functions**
* **Math.sinh(x)** – Hyperbolic sine.
* **Math.cosh(x)** – Hyperbolic cosine.
* **Math.tanh(x)** – Hyperbolic tangent.
---
## **7. Misc Utility Functions**
* **Math.hypot(a, b)** – Returns √(a² + b²) without overflow.
* **Math.copySign(magnitude, sign)** – Copies the sign of one number to another.
* **Math.signum(x)** – Returns sign of x: -1, 0, or +1.
* **Math.nextUp(x)** – Next larger double value.
* **Math.nextDown(x)** – Next smaller double value.
* **Math.nextAfter(a, b)** – Next floating-point value of a towards b.
* **Math.ulp(x)** – Returns the size of the least precision unit of x.
---
## **8. Constants**
* **Math.PI** – Value of π (3.14159...).
* **Math.E** – Value of e (2.71828...).
